Les Cent Contes drolatiques(French, 'The Hundred Facetious Tales'), usually translatedDroll Stories, is a collection of humorous short stories by the French writerHonoré de Balzac, based onGiovanni Boccaccio'sThe Decameronand influenced byFrançois Rabelais. The stories are written in pastiche Renaissance French; although the title promises a hundred, only thirty were published, in groups of ten in 1832, 1833, and 1837.
