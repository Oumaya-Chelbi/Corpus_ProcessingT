Table-Talkis a collection of essays by the English cultural critic and social commentatorWilliam Hazlitt. It was originally published as two volumes, the first of which appeared in April 1821.[2]The essays deal with topics such as art, literature and philosophy.Duncan Wuhas described the essays as the "pinnacle of [Hazlitt's] achievement", and argues thatTable-TalkandThe Plain Speaker(1826) represent Hazlitt's masterpiece.[3]
