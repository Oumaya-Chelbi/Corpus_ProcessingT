"The Battle of the Books" is a short satire written byJonathan Swiftand published as part of theprolegomenato hisA Tale of a Tubin 1704. It depicts a literal battle between books in theKing's Library(housed inSt James's Palaceat the time of the writing), as ideas and authors struggle for supremacy. Because of the satire, "The Battle of the Books" has become a term for theQuarrel of the Ancients and the Moderns. It is one of Swift's earliest well-known works.
