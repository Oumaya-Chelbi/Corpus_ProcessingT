Fables for the Frivolous, orFables for the Frivolous (with Apologies toLa Fontaine), is one of the earliest works by the American parodistGuy Wetmore Carryl. It was published by Harper & Brothers in 1898.[1]Thesefablesare adapted fromJean de La Fontaine's original writings.  TheAesop-style fables are written in verse, and are light-hearted re-tellings of fables from two centuries before, each ending with a moral and a pun. Among the more celebrated of the fables areThe Persevering Tortoise and the Pretentious Hare,The Arrogant Frog and the Superior Bull, andThe Sycophantic Fox and the Gullible Raven.
