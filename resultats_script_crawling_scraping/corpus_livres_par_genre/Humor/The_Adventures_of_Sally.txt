Sally Nicholas is a young, pretty, and popular American woman who lives in aboarding housein New York and works as ataxi dancer. Upon reaching her twenty-first birthday, she inherits a considerable fortune. Sally tries to adjust to her new life, but financial and romantic problems beset her until ahappy endingensues.

Wodehouse biographerRichard UsbornecalledThe Adventures of Sally"a jerky, choppy book... Several short-story themes are tied up untidily together and there is a scrambling of loose ends to finish up."[2]
