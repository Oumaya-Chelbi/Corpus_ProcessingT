A Journal of the Plague Year: Being Observations or Memorials, Of the most Remarkable Occurrences, As well Publick as Private, which happened in London During the last Great Visitation In 1665, commonly calledA Journal of the Plague Year, is a book byDaniel Defoe, first published in March 1722. It is an account of one man's experiences of the year 1665, in which thebubonic plaguestruck the city of London in what became known as theGreat Plague of London, the last epidemic of plague in that city. The book is told somewhat chronologically, though without sections or chapter headings, and with frequent digressions and repetitions.[1]
