Le Morte d'Arthur(originally written asle morte Darthur;Anglo-Norman Frenchfor "The Death of Arthur")[1]is a 15th-centuryMiddle Englishprose reworking by SirThomas Maloryof tales about the legendaryKing Arthur,Guinevere,Lancelot,Merlinand theKnights of the Round Table, along with their respective folklore. In order to tell a "complete" story of Arthur from his conception to his death, Malory compiled, rearranged, interpreted and modified material from various French and English sources. Today, this is one of the best-known works ofArthurian literature. Many authors since the 19th-century revival of the legend have used Malory as their principal source.
